My Trip to Kyoto

By Alex Chen · January 2026

The bamboo grove at Arashiyama felt otherworldly at dawn. Thin shafts of light cut through the towering stalks, and the only sound was the creak of bamboo swaying in the wind.

I had arrived in Kyoto the previous evening, stepping off the Shinkansen into a city that seemed to exist in two centuries at once. Glass-fronted convenience stores sat beneath wooden machiya townhouses, their latticed facades unchanged for generations.

The highlight was Fushimi Inari. Thousands of vermillion torii gates march up Mount Inari in a seemingly endless corridor. At the summit, the city spread below in a grid of grey and green, and I understood why pilgrims had been climbing this path for twelve centuries.
